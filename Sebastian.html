When you start out skating, you'll want to learn the ollie first. This is the most basic trick.
Although some skaters may learn the shove it first, it's less common.

The Ollie

The ollie is the most basic trick because it's the base for all other tricks. The ollie allows you to get your board into the air without grabbing it.
The following images will show the motion of the ollie.




As the image shows, the power the skater gets to raise the board into the air is the rebounding reaction of the tail being pushed to the ground as the skater jumps.
The skater then uses their other foot to slide up against the griptape. This frictive force brings the board up further as the skater raises their legs.

The Shove-It

The shove-it is the second trick any skater should know. A beginner's shove-it is typically a backside spin, which simplified right now is a counterclockwise rotation.
